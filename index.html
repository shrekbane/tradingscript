How the score works

Every confirmed bar, GradeRunner scores the bull side and the bear side independently across five factors. Whichever side is ahead becomes the active direction, and its total becomes the score out of 10.

1

Local trend

Fast EMA vs. slow EMA on your chart's own timeframe. 2 points.

2

Higher-timeframe bias

Same EMA relationship computed on a separate, larger timeframe you choose. 2 points.

3

Momentum (RSI)

Scaled by distance from 50 — a borderline RSI barely moves the score. Up to 2 points.

4

MACD histogram

Confirms momentum direction independently of RSI. 2 points.

5

Trend strength (ADX/DMI)

Only awarded if ADX > 20 — i.e. a real trend actually exists. 2 points.

ScoreGrade
≥ 9 / 10A+
≥ 7 / 10A
≥ 5 / 10B
< 5 / 10C
Always on, not just scored: higher-timeframe agreement is a hard requirement at every grade, including A+ — a signal can't fire against the higher timeframe's bias, even if a sharp local move scores high enough on its own to hit a top grade. This exists specifically to block "buy/sell the bounce against the trend" setups, not to maximize signal count.
MACD agreement is required too: found by comparing factor diagnostics across real B-grade setups — every one had MACD Histogram fully opposed while trend/HTF/ADX all agreed. Every A+/A example checked already had MACD agreeing naturally, so this mainly filters out the weak-momentum B-grade pattern, not top-grade signals.
Entry is delayed until price confirms it, by default: even after the score/HTF/MACD all line up, a signal doesn't fire yet — it's held as "pending" until price rejects a flipped support/resistance zone in the same direction, within a configurable window. No reject in time means no signal at all for that setup. Turn this off (or watch the tracked zones directly, plotted on the chart) if you'd rather trade the moment the confluence lines up.

Built-in risk management

Not just a signal — a full setup, drawn automatically the moment a graded signal fires.

◆

Auto entry / SL / 3 TPs

ATR-based stop loss and three take-profits (2x / 3x / 4x ATR against a 1x ATR stop by default, roughly 2:1, 3:1, 4:1 risk:reward), plotted live on the chart.

▲

Core Signal breakout gate

Optional stricter trigger — price must close beyond the recent N-bar high/low before a signal fires, on top of the grade filter.

〜

Volatility-scaled stops

SL/TP distances automatically widen in High volatility (1.5x) and tighten in Low volatility (0.75x).

⏱

Alert Hours

Restrict alerts to a chosen trading session and timezone — the chart keeps updating 24/7 either way, only notifications are gated.

☰

Minimalist info table

Trade status, HTF trend, score, momentum, trend strength, and volatility — six rows, all live data, theme-adaptive.

↺

Setup history

Optionally keep a trail of past signal badges on the chart so you can audit how recent setups played out.

📊

Grade win-rate stats

A second panel tracks, per grade, how often setups reached TP1 before SL vs. the reverse — pure measurement, no effect on signal logic.

🔐

Breakeven after TP1

The moment TP1 hits, the stop automatically moves to entry — not just a reminder, the tracked SL and drawn line move with it. If price later returns to that level, the alert says "Breakeven hit" instead of "Stop Loss hit."

🔍

Factor diagnostics

Hover any bar in TradingView's Data Window to see each factor's net point contribution — explains exactly which factors carried a past signal's grade, invisible on the chart itself.

🧱

S/R flip confirmation

Tracks the latest swing high/low and, once broken, watches for a reject at the flipped zone (RBS/SBR) before a signal is allowed to fire — on by default, with the tracked zones plottable directly on the chart.

TP3 is intentionally left out of the Buy/Sell alert message — by the time price reaches it, you should already be watching the chart. If price returns to breakeven after TP1 has already hit, the dynamic message says "Breakeven hit" instead of "Stop Loss hit," so a flat exit isn't mistaken for a loss. Point figures use a broker-style convention that varies by instrument: 0.0001 for standard forex pairs, 0.01 for XAUUSD (100 points = $1), and a rough 1.0-per-price-unit approximation for crypto pairs, since "points" isn't a standard crypto concept.

Before you follow a signal

A signal firing means the script's own conditions were met — it doesn't mean every signal is worth taking. Three quick checks before acting on one:

  1. Check the Trend Strength row in the info table. If it reads "Weak," there's no real trend behind the move.
  2. Look at the plotted S/R zone. Is this the first rejection at that level, or has price rejected it there before?
  3. Look for a rejection candle at the zone — marked automatically on the chart via the Engulfing Candle Markers, so you don't have to spot it yourself. Confirms price is actually turning, not just touching the level.

Installation

Not published on TradingView's public script library — add it manually via the Pine Editor.

  1. Open GradeRunner.pine in the repo and copy the full contents (use the "Raw" button on GitHub, then select all + copy).
  2. In TradingView, open any chart and click the Pine Editor tab at the bottom of the screen.
  3. Click Open → New blank script, select all the placeholder code, and delete it.
  4. Paste in the copied script.
  5. Click Save, name it, then click Add to Chart.
  6. Open the indicator's settings (⚙️) to configure it for your instrument and timeframe.
Tip: pair your chart timeframe with the higher-timeframe input at roughly a 6–12x ratio. A 60min HTF bias works well on a 5–15min chart; drop it to 15–30min if you're scalping on 1–5min charts.
